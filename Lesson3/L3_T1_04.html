Stay/Become Heat Tolerant

4 of 5

When workers are exposed to hot work environments, they readily show signs of heat stress and/or heat-related illness. On repeated exposure to a hot environment, there is a marked adaptation in which the principal physiologic benefit appears to result from an increased sweating efficiency evidenced by earlier onset of sweating, greater sweat production, lower electrolyte concentration, and a concomitant stabilization of the circulation. After daily heat exposure for 7-14 days, most individuals perform work with a much lower core temperature and heart rate and a higher sweat rate with none of the symptoms that were originally experienced.

  • 1
  • 2
  • Lack of acclimatization has been shown to be a major factor associated with worker heat-related illness and death.

  • For workers with previous experience, the acclimatization regimen should be no more than:

    Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4
    50% exposure 60% exposure 80% exposure 100% exposure

  • For new workers the schedule should be no more than 20% exposure on day 1, and an increase of no more than 20% each additional day.
  • Being physically fit can enhance heat tolerance for both heat-acclimatized and non-acclimatized workers. The time required for non-physically fit individuals to develop acclimatization is about 50% greater than for the physically fit.
  • Acclimatized workers lose less salt in their sweat than non-acclimatized workers.
  • Air conditioning will not affect acclimatization.
  • Workers can maintain their acclimatization even if they are away from the job for a few days; however, if they are away for a week or more then there may be a significant loss of acclimatization. It can often be regained in 2-3 days.
  • Sudden seasonal shifts and sudden increases in environmental temperatures may result in cases of heat-related illness even for acclimatized workers.
  • Although most healthy workers will be able to acclimatize, some workers may be heat intolerant. Often this is not discovered until after they suffer a heat-related illness. These individuals will remain intolerant to heat; acclimatization will not help them.
